The Rules:
Any live cell with fewer than two live neighbours of its own species dies, as if caused by under-population.
Any live cell with two or three live neighbours of its own species lives on to the next generation.
However, any live cell with more than three live neighbours of any species dies, as if by overcrowding.
Any dead cell with exactly three live neighbours of one species becomes a live cell, as if by reproduction.
However, if a dead cell has exactly three live neighbors of 2 different species, the cell stays dead, as if by inability to decide which species to become.
